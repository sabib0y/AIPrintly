# AIPrintly Business Model & Monetisation Research

## Context

I'm building **AIPrintly**, an AI-powered print-on-demand platform where users can:
1. Upload images or generate them using AI
2. Customise products (mugs, t-shirts, prints, canvas)
3. Create personalised children's storybooks with AI-generated stories and illustrations
4. Purchase and have items fulfilled via Printful (merchandise) and Blurb (books)

I need to develop a sustainable, profitable business model. This research should inform pricing strategy, monetisation approach, and competitive positioning.

## Research Objectives

1. Understand how competitors monetise similar offerings
2. Identify industry-standard margins in print-on-demand
3. Determine optimal pricing models for AI creative tools
4. Analyse the personalised children's book market specifically
5. Recommend a monetisation strategy that balances profitability with user acquisition

## Additional Context

**My constraints:**
- AI generation costs me approximately £0.02-0.04 per image
- I want at least 50% margin on AI costs (but willing to subsidise if products make up for it)
- Fulfilment is via Printful (merch) and Blurb (books) — I don't control base costs
- Target market: UK consumers, initially
- MVP launching soon — need a simple model that can evolve

**My hypothesis (to validate or challenge):**
- AI generation should be cheap/generous to reduce friction
- Main profit comes from product margins
- Storybooks could be a premium, higher-margin product
- Subscription might be overkill for MVP — credits simpler

Please validate or challenge this with data.
